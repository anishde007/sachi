PROFILE & JOURNEY

With over 35 years in Animal Biotechnology, I have dedicated my career to understanding the genomic architecture of Indian livestock. From my early days at BCKV to my current role as National Professor at NDRI, my focus has remained constant: rigorous science for sustainable agriculture.

My work involves characterizing genetic diversity, developing simple field-deployable DNA isolation processes, and understanding the molecular basis of innate immunity in large animals. I have also been deeply involved in mitigating antimicrobial resistance, tracing pathogen transmission from animal to human food chains.

RESEARCH AREAS

GENOMICS & DIVERSITY

Studying genetic diversity in Indian cattle and buffalo. Key work involves haplotyping innate immune genes and developing genome information resources to support breeding programs.

RUMINANT IMMUNOLOGY

Deciphering the molecular basis of innate immunity. Researching DNA and RNA sensors in animal cells and developing fecundity vaccines.

AMR MITIGATION

Understanding how bacterial AMR pathogens move from animal to human food chains. Developing therapeutic approaches against MRSA and mapping resistance patterns.
